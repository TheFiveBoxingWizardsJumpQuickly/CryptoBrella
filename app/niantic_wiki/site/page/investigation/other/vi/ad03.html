The node


Ingredients

1.5 kg brussel sprouts 250 g diced bacon 100 g sliced almonds 30 g crushed garlic 30 ml balsamic vinegar seasoning

Recipe

Fry the bacon until browned and slightly crisp and remove from pan. Add the crushed garlic and the almonds to the heated bacon fat and cook briefly until lightly roasted. Add the brussel sprouts and toss to cover them with oil. Add the balsamic vinegar and cook the brussel sprouts until brown, stirring frequently. Remove from heat, stir in the bacon and season to taste.

Adjusting the levels of the image, the following text pops out:

23.3N, 29.2W, 27.9N, 87.4E, 22.8S, 122.1E, 29.9N, 41.6W, 19.8S, 69.1W, 44.9S, 41.5E, 14.2S, 151.9W, 32.5S, 5.2W

Eight pairs of coordinates. The coordinates are only accurate to one tenth of a degree, and don’t seem to point to anything interesting on earth. However, the image is of a statue of Helios and Selene, the Sun god and Moon goddess (this is fairly easy to find - a Google Image search of the image will yield this result directly). With this in mind, interpret the coordinates as Selenic Coordinates. One could use any sufficiently detailed moon atlas to look up these coordinates, but searching for the coordinates Verbatim in Google gives the solution very easily: each set of coordinates points towards a crater:

23.3N 29.2W Euler 27.9N, 87.4E Urey 22.8S, 122.1E Xenophon 29.9N 41.6W Angstrom 19.8S, 69.1W Darwin 44.9S, 41.5E Janssen 14.2S, 151.9W Galois 32.5S, 5.2W Deslandres

Taking the first letters of each crater name in order yields EUXADJGD → 5ux14jg4 The picture is a statue of of Helios on Selene → statue5ux14jg4
