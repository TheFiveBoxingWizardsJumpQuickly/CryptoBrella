Oct 03 2013

P.A. Chapeau Investigation Board Release

Edgar Allen Wright G+ Post

Original Link

I have spent some time studying the secret language of myth.

Read more

It is my qualified opinion that nearly all myths hide a deeper truth. For instance, the Golden Fleece was not literally a 'lambs skin' or whatever the ridiculous legend was, but it was indeed the wheat-fields of Asia up the Dardanelles. Very necessary to sustain a population in the mediterranean and a secret trade route to Asia. Remember, in those days there were no maps. Many epics contained coded information as to where valuable things were located. For instance Scylla and Charybdis in the Odyssey might well have been the secret location of Tin resources... The strategic metal of the Bronze Age...

And one thing I have always found to be true in mythology, where there is a monster, there is a treasure. Monsters are always there to protect something.

Is this not also true in the modern world?
