An Eye to the Future

<img src="/media/resources/thoughts_for_the_day/totd0611.png" alt="totd0611.png" loading="lazy" width="300"> 2013-06-11

>This leaked NIA memo sheds light on the Portal Mods emerging from the competing XM tech corporations, IQTech, Visur and Hulong Transglobal, but it also provides us with an update on some people we've been keeping track of.

Dr. Devra Bogdanovich, of course, is now working with Visur, headed by the Russian billionaire businessman Ilya Pevtsov. Read Felicia's novel for a better understanding of how that relationship came to be.

Dr. Oliver Lynton-Wolfe, Devra's old nemesis and the first true XM tech pioneer at the Niantic Facility in Geneva, is now operating under IQTech, the private US-based contractor run by Dr. Ezekiel Calvin, former lead of the Niantic Project.

We still don't know who exactly the scientific talent behind the Chinese mega-corporation Hulong Transglobal's XM labs are, but their foray into the Congo chasing Hank Johnson (read Alignment:Ingress by Thomas Greanias for those details) assured them an early lead in Dark XM research.

The final piece of info, suspected but never confirmed, is that the brutal, cold, dangerous former head of Niantic Security, Jay Phillips, has taken over Yuen Ni's position as head of the NIA. Last seen being given carte-blanche to clean up the leaks and chaos surround Niantic, it seems he has maneuvered that opportunity to the top of the shadowy intelligence agency.

Raising the Stares

<img src="/media/resources/thoughts_for_the_day/totd0623.png" alt="totd0623.png" loading="lazy" width="300"> 2013-06-23

>Hardball.

After receiving the #timezero package (1,2) on behalf of the Enlightened, Agent Richard Walden uploaded this video, with claims that he has destroyed it in order to prevent it from falling into the hands of the three XM corporations currently competing for control of its contents: Visur, IQTech and Hulong Transglobal.

If true, it's a bold move, and one with unknown consequences. From what I can gather, the package contained some kind of visual evidence of symbols, glyphs with great power, that were recovered by Hank Johnson from a mine (1,2) near the Texas/Mexico border. If the lost letters Hank recovered are to be believed, the glyphs may indeed have had influence on those who saw them.

A series of videos uploaded by Carrie Campbell showed Hank on the run during his journey to retrieve these symbols, being chased by Private Military Special Ops, possibly operatives from Visur, Hulong, IQTech, or all three.

Here is the list of questions running through my mind:

Did Richard Walden actually destroy the package? Or is this leverage... Does Hank Johnson have a backup of the contents? Does another copy exist? Was the courier who transported the package from Paris to London clean, or was she a double agent for someone? If so, who? Do they have a copy of the contents? Last we heard of Carrie Campbell, she fled from the package handoff at #TimeZero. Why couldn't she take it? Was she being followed? Is she safe?

It's been a strange week, that's for sure.
