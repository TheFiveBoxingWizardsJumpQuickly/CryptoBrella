The Niantic Calamity (12/2012)

12/01/2012

BLACK OPERATIONS
Covert operations by an intelligence agency that have no official owner, and can not be attributed to the organization in charge of them for security reasons.

THEORY OF EVERYTHING
A theory that fully explains and links together all known physical phenomena, and can predict the outcome of any experiment.

An early CIA 'mind control' program. The precursor to MKULTRA.

12/08/2012

DEAD DROP
A way of using a secret location to exchange a physical object or piece of information during an espionage operation. The opposite of a live drop..

12/09/2012

CRYPTOMNESIA
Inadvertently plagiarizing somebody or something because you've forgotten the source. One of the most famous cases of cryptomnesia was of Paul McCartney going around asking people if they'd ever heard `Yesterday' before because it came so easily that he was convinced that he must have heard it before.operation.

12/10/2012

COMPROMISED
When a secret operative's identity or operation is uncovered and cannot continue as planned.

12/11/2012

REMOTE VIEWING
The paranormal ability of a subject to have an out-of-body view of a person, place, or moment in time without being physically present there.

12/12/2012

FUGUE STATE
A mental state associated with a self-disappearance.

12/13/2012

SQUID
Superconducting QUantum Interference Device Extremely sensitive detectors used by physicists to detect quantum level changes in magnetic flux.

12/14/2012

RATIOCINATION
Ordered chain of logical thinking.

12/15/2012

ASEMIC WRITING
A wordless form of writing.

12/16/2012

BLOWBACK
The unexpected, and most often negative consequences experienced as the result of an intelligence operation.

12/17/2012

THE RABBIT
In surveillance: The target of an operation.

12/18/2012

WAVE-FUNCTION COLLAPSE
All particles exist as waves that contain information about many states that the particle can simultaneously be in until observation or measurement; at this point, all of the possible states of the particle are excluded except one randomly chosen one, which is the one observed.

12/19/2012

TURING TEST
Named after Alan Turing. A test to determine if a computer can exhibit intelligence that is indistinguishable from a human.

12/20/2012

MKULTRA
A notorious and massive CIA operation lasting 20 years with the goal of exploring 'behavioral engineering of humans.'

12/21/2012

NEOLITHIC REVOLUTION
~8000 BCE. The advent of agriculture, and the beginning of human civilization.

12/22/2012

ANFRACTUOSITY
Having the quality of being winding, circuitous, or intricate.

12/23/2012

MOLE
An agent within an organization who is tasked to disrupt the goals of that organization via intelligence leaks or sabotage.

12/24/2012

PROVOCATEUR
An agent sent into a group of people with the objective of causing disruption and embarassment.

12/25/2012

PRETERNATURAL
Outside of the ordinary course of nature. Exceptional or abnormal.

Differs from 'supernatural' in that preternatural phenomena are believed to have rational explanations.

12/26/2012

TRANSDUCTION
The conversion of a stimulus from one form to another as it travels a neural pathway.

12/27/2012

AMBIGUITY
A perception that can be interpreted in more than one way.

12/28/2012

AGENT OF INFLUENCE
An agent in a position of power who uses their influence and status to benefit the country or agency to whom they belong.
